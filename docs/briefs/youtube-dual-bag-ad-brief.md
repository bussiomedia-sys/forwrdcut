# FORWRD — YouTube Ad Production Brief: "Two Bags, One Standard"

**For:** Claude Code (video editor)
**Deliverable:** One YouTube ad featuring BOTH bags (Court Ranger V2 + Court Caddy), shown off and transitioned through, with strong overlay text + voiceover.
**Built from:** FORWRD ad-producer skill (archetypes, messaging angles, brand context) + Dusty's Facebook Ads Playbook + master marketing instructions.

---

## 0. READ THIS FIRST — Platform mechanics (YouTube ≠ Meta)

This is a **YouTube** ad. The rules differ from our Meta creative. Build for these:

- **The 5-second skip wall.** On skippable in-stream ads, the Skip button appears at 0:05. Everything that matters for a skipper must land before 0:05: a scroll-stopping visual, a reason to keep watching, and ideally the brand/product on screen. Treat 0:00–0:05 as a self-contained mini-ad.
- **Sound is ON by default.** Unlike Meta's silent feed, YouTube plays with audio. The **voiceover is the spine** — it carries the message. Overlay text is *reinforcement and emphasis*, not the primary channel. (This matches what you already have: strong VO + strong overlays.)
- **Front-load the brand.** Say/show "FORWRD" inside the first 5 seconds. Skippers should at least leave knowing the name.
- **Two CTAs.** One soft verbal CTA mid-roll (for engaged viewers who'll skip soon), one hard CTA at the end (for full-watch viewers). End card with logo + offer + URL.
- **Build 2 lengths from one edit:** a **:30** (primary, in-stream skippable) and a **:15** (bumper/shorts-style cutdown). The :15 is the first 5s hook + the strongest single bag beat + end card. Edit the :30 first, then derive the :15.

## 1. The concept

**Archetype:** Listicle / Feature Breakdown hybrid → "Two Bags, One Standard" structure.
We're not doing a story arc. We're doing a **fast, premium product showcase** that transitions between the two bags, using big overlay callouts on each hero feature, with confident VO tying them together under the "Never Settle" standard.

**Why this archetype:** Per the playbook, listicle/feature-breakdown formats are algorithmically readable, fast to watch, and let us pack semantic variety (durability + organization + style) into one asset. It's also the cleanest way to show TWO products in 30 seconds without it feeling like two separate ads bolted together.

**Lead messaging angle:** **Durability + Style** ("built to last AND looks this good"), with **Organization/Function** as the supporting angle. We lead with the visual (premium look), prove with the build (materials), and the dual-bag format itself sells the "there's a FORWRD for every player" idea.

**The unifying spine (the line everything hangs on):**
> "Two bags. One standard. Never Settle."

The creative tension we're exploiting: most pickleball bags are an afterthought from a paddle company. FORWRD is the only brand that does *only* bags — and we have two, engineered to the same obsessive standard, at two price points. The ad's job is to make a viewer think *"these look like $300 camera bags, not pickleball bags"* and then *"there's one for me."*

**Alt hooks (give the editor options — produce the primary, keep these as B-roll variants for testing):**

## 4. Overlay text rules (this is the "strong overlay" you asked for)

- **Font:** Barlow Condensed 900 or Druk Wide Heavy (brand headline fonts). All-caps.
- **Color:** White on footage; switch to **orange #EA6024** for the single most important word in a callout if you want punch (e.g. **MODULAR** in orange, rest white). End-card accent is orange.
- **Placement:** Center or lower-third. **Keep all text inside the center 80% of frame** so it survives YouTube's UI chrome and any reframing.
- **One idea per card.** Never stack two callouts. The overlay names the feature; the VO explains it. They reinforce, they don't duplicate verbatim.
- **Timing:** Overlay hits *on the VO emphasis word*, not before. Punch in (fast scale-up or hard cut), hold, hard cut out. No slow dissolves — keep it snappy and premium.
- **Motion:** Subtle. A hard cut or a 2-frame scale punch reads more premium than a slide or spin. Match the confident, understated brand tone.

---

## 5. Voiceover direction

- **Tone:** Confident, grounded, a little irreverent — "a friend who happens to know everything about pickleball gear." Enthusiasm yes, hype no. Show-don't-tell.
- **Pace:** Brisk but not rushed. Let the pivot question (shot 5) and the final "Never Settle" land with a beat of space.
- **Voice:** Male or female both work; lean toward a warm, assured read, not an announcer. (If generating via ElevenLabs, pick a natural conversational voice over a "commercial VO" voice.)
- **Emphasis words** (these are where overlays sync): *afterthought, ballistic nylon, every, lighter, everything, lifetime, switch, standard.*
- **Hard rule — brand voice:** No em dashes in any on-screen copy. Don't say "revolutionary," "disruptive," "game-changer," "luxury," or claim "best." Name specific materials and features instead. Use "we/our" — we're part of the community.

## 6. Footage / editing notes for the editor

You already have the content library. Pull and prioritize:
- **Macro/detail shots** for the hook and materials beats: ballistic nylon weave, YKK zipper pulls, Hypalon pulls, magnetic ball pocket snapping, stitching close-ups.
- **Upright-standing shots** of both bags (a signature FORWRD design cue — use it).
- **Caddy specifics:** modular paddle sleeve velcro in/out, dual magnetic ball pockets (the "standout feature, hands down" per reviewers), fence hooks, magnetic bottle pockets.
- **Ranger specifics:** front-opening reveal with gear visible, on-shoulder walking shot, fence-hook hang, the leaner silhouette.
- **The transition (shot 5)** is the hinge of the whole ad. Use a clean whip-pan, a match-cut on a similar motion, or a hard graphic wipe in brand orange. This is where "Caddy → Ranger" reads as *one lineup*, not two ads.
- **End card:** black background, FORWRD logo, forwrd.co, orange accent, lifetime-warranty line. Hold 2s+.

**Pacing check:** YouTube rewards clarity. If a beat feels slow in the middle, trim it — better to be a hair too fast than to give a skipper a reason to leave.

Bumper / shorts-style. Structure:

## 9. Production sequence (how to run this)

1. Lock the **script + VO** first (Section 3 + 5). Audio is the spine — every cut length comes from the audio.
2. Generate VO, **measure exact segment durations**, lock the timeline.
3. Build the shot list against locked durations, pull clips from the library.
4. Edit the **:30** primary. Add overlays synced to VO emphasis.
5. Review → revise pacing/text/transitions.
6. Derive the **:15** cutdown.
7. Export both. (If running paid: per the playbook, this is ONE net-new asset — pair it with 2–3 other distinct concepts in the ad set rather than 3 hook-variants of this same video.)
